Subject-Wise Strategy & Resources

Physics

  1. Learn the concept from NCERT or coaching notes first
  2. Solve all NCERT examples and back exercises
  3. Solve H.C. Verma Concepts of Physics (Volume 1 and 2)
  4. Solve JEE Main Previous Year Questions (last 10 years)
  5. Attempt timed mixed-topic question sets

Key Books: NCERT, H.C. Verma (Vol 1 & 2), Arihant JEE Main PYQ Book

Chemistry

Inorganic Chemistry

  • NCERT is your ONLY primary resource
  • Read each chapter line-by-line, multiple times
  • Create short notes for quick revision

Organic Chemistry

  • Master General Organic Chemistry (GOC) first
  • Memorize all named reactions and mechanisms
  • Practice daily reaction-based questions

Physical Chemistry

  • Practice numericals regularly
  • Speed comes only through repetition

Key Books: NCERT (mandatory for Inorganic), O.P. Tandon for Physical, MS Chouhan for Organic, JEE Main PYQ Book

Mathematics

  1. Start with NCERT for concept clarity
  2. Progress to coaching material or R.S. Aggarwal
  3. Solve JEE Main PYQs extensively
  4. Attempt mock tests under timed conditions

For Calculus: Understand the "why" behind formulas. Don't memorize blindly. Practice is the only key to speed.

Key Books: NCERT, Coaching Material or R.S. Aggarwal for JEE Main, JEE Main PYQ Book

Golden Rule: You don't need 5 different books per subject. Finishing 2–3 quality resources thoroughly is far more effective than collecting many and finishing none.

Error Notebook System

Maintain one dedicated notebook for all mock test mistakes. After every mock, categorize each mistake:

CategoryWhat to RecordAction
Conceptual ErrorTopic name, specific concept misunderstoodRe-read theory, solve 10 similar questions
Formula MistakeCorrect formula, conditions where it appliesAdd to formula sheet, revise daily for a week
Silly ErrorNature of error (misread, calculation, sign error)Note pattern; if repeating, slow down reading
Time ManagementQuestion type that consumed extra timePractice similar questions with a stopwatch

Review this notebook every alternate day. The goal is to never repeat the same mistake twice.

Why Aim for 98 Percentile, Not 95?

  • If you prepare for 98 percentile, even an average exam day leaves you at 95–97 percentile.
  • If you prepare only for 95 percentile, a bad day, tougher paper, or a few silly mistakes can easily drop you below your target.
  • Aim higher to land safely.
